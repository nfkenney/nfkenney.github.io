Cooking: Recipe reviews

My new year's resolution was to cook more as both a health-conscious and money-saving measure. As a result, I'm aggregating all the recipes I've tried, as well as what I thought about about each one (and pictures, if I took them).

Review Table

Day Name Rating Comments Modifications Recipe Link
Day 1 Pad Krapow Gai (Spicy Thai Basil Chicken) 8/10 Nice and spicy. Lots of flavor. Slightly overcooked the veggies, but they still tasted great. I didn't have soy sauce or oyster sauce, so I added Sunny's Korean Teriyaki Sauce and extra fish sauce instead. Also added some Sambal Oelek for extra spice as I couldn't find Thai chilis at Kroger. Recipe
Day 2 Pork Fried Rice 7/10 Good. Pork was flavorful and not too overcooked. Peas were a little overdone. Korean Teriyaki sauce went great in this recipe. Peas were almost burned but still somehow tasted really good? Didn't have soy sauce or fresh ginger, so I went with Korean Teriyaki and a bit less Sambal Oelek than yesterday and it worked here as well. Also didn't have broccoli Recipe
Day 3 One Pan Orecchiette Pasta 2/10 Really not great, and about half that was my fault. The pan I used was too small, which resulted in uneven pasta cooking, but pretty much all the flavor in the sauce is from low-sodium chicken stock, which is both too thin and too watery for a proper pasta sauce. Didn't have arugala or orecchiette (I used regular bowties instead), both of which which might help somewhat, but I wonder if the dish really gets much better than this. Recipe
Day 4 Golden Curry 7/10 This one isn't technically full cooking, as I did use a spice mix(this one, to be specific), but it still involved enough cooking that I felt it counted. I chopped all ingredients, before crisping the vegetables and browning the chicken. I then followed the instructions on the curry pack (properly add 2 and a quarter cups of water and the sauce packet and stir until curry is properly mixed) I made this with carrots, an onion, chicken thighs, and a potato. Recipe

Day 4 Long Day's Breakfast 6/10 This one stretches the limits of cooking as well, as it was just a simply egg scramble, but making food from scratch is making food from scratch, and so it counts. This version of an egg scramble contained 3 eggs, sharp cheddar cheese, grilled onions (white and green), grilled mushrooms, and spicy Italian sausage. Recipe: Add everything but eggs to pan over medium heat. Sautee until golden brown. Add eggs to pan and scramble.
